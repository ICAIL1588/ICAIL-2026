Article Non-qualifying Operations
A good shall not be considered to satisfy the
requirement of change in tariff classification or specific
manufacturing or processing operation set out in Annex merely by having undergone following operations:
(a) operations to ensure the preservation of products
in good condition during transport and storage

(b) changes of packaging and breaking up and assembly

(c) disassembly;
(d) placing in bottles, cases, boxes and other simple

(e) collection of parts and components classified as
a good pursuant to Rule 2(a) of the General Rules

(f) mere making-up of sets of articles; or
(g) any combination of operations referred to in
subparagraphs (a) through (f) above.

reloading or any other operation to preserve it

Each Party shall ensure that all relevant information
of general application pertaining to its customs laws is
readily available to any interested person.

Article Good Offices, Conciliation or Mediation
Good offices, conciliation or mediation may be
requested at any time by either Party. They may begin at
any time if the Parties agree and, at the request of either
Party, be terminated at any time.
If the Parties agree, good offices, conciliation or
mediation may continue while procedures of the arbitral
tribunal provided for in this Chapter are in progress.
Article Establishment of Arbitral Tribunals
The complaining Party that requested consultations
under Article may request in writing the establishment
of an arbitral tribunal to the Party complained against:
(a) if the Party complained against does not enter
into such consultations within days after the
date of receipt of the request for consultations
under that Article; or
(b) if the Parties fail to resolve the dispute
through such consultations under that Article
within days after the date of receipt of the
request for such consultations,
provided that the complaining Party considers that any
benefit accruing to it directly or indirectly under this
Agreement is being nullified or impaired as a result of the
failure of the Party complained against to carry out its
obligations, or as a result of the application by the Party
complained against of measures which are in conflict with
the obligations of that Party, under this Agreement.
Any request for the establishment of an arbitral
tribunal pursuant to this Article shall identify:
(a) the specific measures at issue; and
(b) the legal basis of the complaint including the
provisions of this Agreement alleged to have been
breached and any other relevant provisions.
Each Party shall within days after the date of
receipt of the request for the establishment of an arbitral
tribunal, appoint one arbitrator who may be its national
and propose up to candidates to serve as the third
arbitrator who shall be the chair of the arbitral tribunal.
The third arbitrator shall not be a national of either
Party, nor have his or her usual place of residence in
either Party, nor be employed by either Party.
The Parties shall agree on and appoint the third
arbitrator within days after the date of receipt of the
request for the establishment of an arbitral tribunal,
taking into account the candidates proposed pursuant to
paragraph above.
If a Party has not appointed one arbitrator pursuant
to paragraph above, or if the Parties fail to agree on
and appoint the third arbitrator pursuant to paragraph above, such arbitrator or such third arbitrator shall be
chosen by lot within a further period of days from the
candidates proposed pursuant to paragraph above.
The arbitral tribunal should be composed of
arbitrators with relevant technical or legal expertise.
Article Functions of Arbitral Tribunals
The arbitral tribunal established pursuant to Article
(a) should consult with the Parties as appropriate
and provide adequate opportunities for the
development of a mutually satisfactory
resolution;
(b) shall make its award in accordance with this
Agreement and applicable rules of international
law;
(c) shall set out, in its award, its findings of law
and fact, together with the reasons therefor; and
(d) may, apart from giving its findings, include in
its award suggested implementation options for
the Parties to consider in conjunction with
Article The arbitral tribunal may seek, from the Parties, such
relevant information as it considers necessary and
appropriate. The Parties shall respond promptly and fully
to any request by an arbitral tribunal for such
information.
The arbitral tribunal may seek information from any
relevant source and may consult experts to obtain their
Opinion on certain aspects of the matter. With respect to
factual issues concerning a scientific or other technical
matter raised by a Party, the arbitral tribunal may request
advisory reports in writing from an expert or experts. The
arbitral tribunal may, at the request of a Party or on its
own initiative, select, in consultation with the Parties,
no fewer than scientific or technical experts who shall
assist the arbitral tribunal throughout its proceedings,
but who shall not have the right to vote in respect of any
decision to be made by the arbitral tribunal, including its
award.
The award of the arbitral tribunal shall be drafted
without the presence of the Parties, and in the light of
the information provided and the statements made.
The arbitral tribunal shall, within days after the
date of its establishment, submit to the Parties its draft
award, including both descriptive part and its findings and
conclusions, for the purpose of enabling the Parties to
review precise aspects of the draft award. When the
arbitral tribunal considers that it cannot submit to the
Parties its draft award within the afore-mentioned days
period, it may extend that period with the consent of the
Parties. However, in no case should the period from the
establishment of the arbitral tribunal to the submission of
the draft award to the Parties exceed days. A Party
may submit comments in writing to the arbitral tribunal on
the draft award within days after the date of submission
of the draft award.
The arbitral tribunal shall issue its award, within days after the date of submission of the draft award.
The arbitral tribunal shall attempt to make its
decisions, including its award, by consensus but may also
make such decisions, including its award, by majority vote.
The award of the arbitral tribunal shall be fin al and
binding on the Parties.
Article Proceedings of Arbitral Tribunals
The arbitral tribunal shall meet in closed session.
The deliberations of the arbitral tribunal, the
documents submitted to it and the draft award referred to
in paragraph of Article shall be kept confidential.
Notwithstanding paragraph above, either Party may
make public statements as to its views regarding the
dispute, but shall treat as confidential, information and
written submissions submitted by the other Party to the
arbitral tribunal which that other Party has designated as
confidential. Where a Party has provided information or
written submissions designated to be confidential, the
other Party may request a non-confidential summary of the
information or written submissions which may be disclosed
publicly. The Party to whom such a request is made may
agree to such a request and submit such a summary, or deny
the request without providing any reasons or justification.
The Parties shall be given the opportunity to attend
any of the presentations, statements or rebuttals in the
proceeding. Any information or written submissions
submitted by a Party to the arbitral tribunal, including
any comments on the descriptive part of the draft award and
responses to questions put by the arbitral tribunal, shall
be made available to the other Party.
Article Termination of Proceedings
The Parties may agree to terminate the proceedings of
the arbitral tribunal at any time by jointly so notifying
the chair of the arbitral tribunal.

the expiry of that implementation period, enter into

Satisfactory compensation has been agreed within days

complaining Party may notify the Party complained against
that it intends to suspend the application of concessions
or other obligations under this Agreement.
If the complaining Party considers that the Party
complained against has failed to comply with the award
within the implementation period as specified pursuant to
paragraph above, it may refer the matter to an arbitral
tribunal. If the arbitral tribunal confirms that the Party
complained against has failed to comply with the award

paragraph above, the complaining Party may, within days after the date of such confirmation by the arbitral
tribunal, notify the Party complained against that it

obligations under this Agreement.

Article Expenses
Unless the Parties agree otherwise, the expenses of
the arbitral tribunal, including the remuneration of the
arbitrators, shall be borne by the Parties in equal shares.
